Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2026

JR Recovery Process Service ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of the information entrusted to us by our clients, courts, and the individuals we interact with during the course of our investigations, process serving, and asset recovery operations. This policy complies with applicable New York State and federal privacy regulations.

1. Information We Collect
  • Client Information: Names, business details, billing addresses, and contact information of the law firms, creditors, or individuals hiring us.
  • Subject Information: Data necessary to execute legal process or recover assets, including target names, known addresses, vehicle identification numbers (VINs), employment details, and skip-tracing data obtained through lawful investigative databases.
  • Digital Data: Standard website analytics, IP addresses, and communication logs submitted through our secure online portals.
2. How We Use Information

Information is utilized strictly for the execution of contracted services. This includes locating individuals for the service of legal documents (summons, subpoenas, family court documents), tracking and recovering collateral/assets, executing court-ordered evictions, and providing notarized affidavits of service to the court. We do not sell, rent, or lease personal data to third parties for marketing purposes under any circumstances.

3. Information Sharing & Disclosure

We only disclose information when legally required or necessary to fulfill a contract. This includes sharing affidavits of service with the respective courts, returning recovery data to the contracted creditor, or cooperating with local law enforcement agencies when mandated by law.

4. Data Security & Retention

Led by a former law enforcement officer, our agency treats data security with the highest operational priority. Physical documents are secured in locked, restricted-access facilities. Digital data is encrypted and stored on secure servers. Data related to completed cases is retained only as long as legally required by New York State regulations regarding process server record-keeping, after which it is securely destroyed.

For inquiries regarding this policy, contact JR Recovery at jrrecovery12@gmail.com.
